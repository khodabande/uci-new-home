About Me

My name is Hadi Khodabandeh (persian: هادی خدابنده, click here for pronunciation), I am a second year Ph.D. student of Computer Science here in University of California, Irvine. My area of study is theoretical computer science (TCS) and my focus is on graph theory and computational geometry. I am honoured to be advised by professor David Eppstein.

I was working on Greedy Spanners for a while, which led to a paper on the edge crossings of the greedy spanner. You can read more about this result in this blog post by professor Eppstein. Now I am working on different problems together with the other students in the theory group.

You can always find me in the theory office, DBH 4099, or by contacting me via khodabah at uci dot edu.

I love traveling and playing piano, checkout my instagram @hadi_kdb or my sound cloud hadi_kh!
